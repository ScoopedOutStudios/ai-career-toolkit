# Role Expectation Rubric — Software Engineering IC Track

Derived from public engineering career frameworks (StaffEng, GitLab, Monzo progression model, LeadDev synthesis).

## How To Use

- Score each artifact, story, or role on a 1-5 scale for each dimension.
- Use to evaluate your readiness for a target level.
- Use to assess whether a role opportunity offers growth in key dimensions.
- Re-evaluate monthly as interview feedback and career evidence accumulate.

## Scoring Scale

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 1 | No evidence or exposure |
| 2 | Some exposure, limited ownership |
| 3 | Demonstrated competency with clear examples |
| 4 | Strong, consistent performance with measurable impact |
| 5 | Exceptional — recognized as a go-to person in this dimension |

## Dimensions

### 1) Scope of Impact

How broad is the blast radius of your work?

- **Mid-level:** Feature or component-level outcomes within a team.
- **Senior:** Team-level outcomes with clear ownership of a subsystem or product area.
- **Staff:** Multi-team or area-level outcomes that shape technical direction.
- **Sr Staff:** Collective-level outcomes spanning multiple teams or organizational boundaries.
- **Principal:** Org or company-level trajectory changes with multi-quarter strategic horizon.

### 2) Ambiguity Handling

How well do you operate without complete requirements?

- **Mid-level:** Executes well-defined tasks; asks clarifying questions.
- **Senior:** Breaks down ambiguous problems into actionable plans.
- **Staff:** Frames options, tradeoffs, and phased execution for ambiguous domains.
- **Sr Staff:** Creates clarity from chaos; defines the problem space for others.
- **Principal:** Identifies and structures problems that others haven't recognized yet.

### 3) Technical Direction

Do you set direction or follow it?

- **Mid-level:** Implements within established patterns.
- **Senior:** Proposes improvements to existing architecture.
- **Staff:** Sets architecture direction for a domain; balances delivery and platform health.
- **Sr Staff:** Defines technical strategy across multiple domains.
- **Principal:** Shapes company-wide technical vision and multi-year technical roadmap.

### 4) Cross-Functional Influence

How effectively do you drive alignment without authority?

- **Mid-level:** Collaborates within engineering team.
- **Senior:** Works effectively with PM, design, and adjacent teams.
- **Staff:** Aligns product, engineering, security, and infrastructure stakeholders.
- **Sr Staff:** Drives decisions across orgs without relying on formal authority.
- **Principal:** Influences executive decisions and company-wide technical strategy.

### 5) Business Outcome Linkage

Do you connect technical work to business impact?

- **Mid-level:** Understands product goals; delivers assigned features.
- **Senior:** Explains how technical choices affect product metrics.
- **Staff:** Connects technical choices to measurable business impact.
- **Sr Staff:** Uses metrics beyond output (risk, reliability, cost, velocity, adoption).
- **Principal:** Drives technical strategy based on business trajectory and market dynamics.

### 6) Multiplication Effect

Do you make others more effective?

- **Mid-level:** Helps teammates through code review and pair programming.
- **Senior:** Mentors junior engineers; improves team practices.
- **Staff:** Mentors/sponsors engineers and raises team capability.
- **Sr Staff:** Creates reusable standards, patterns, or systems that scale beyond their team.
- **Principal:** Establishes organizational capabilities that outlast their direct involvement.

### 7) Operational Quality

Is reliability designed in or bolted on?

- **Mid-level:** Follows operational standards; handles assigned on-call.
- **Senior:** Designs for reliability; contributes to incident response.
- **Staff:** Reliability, observability, and incident readiness are designed into their work.
- **Sr Staff:** Raises operational bar across teams; leads post-incident improvements.
- **Principal:** Shapes org-wide operational culture and standards.

### 8) AI-Native Practice

How effectively do you leverage AI tools in engineering work?

- **Mid-level:** Uses AI coding assistants for individual productivity.
- **Senior:** Integrates AI tools into personal workflow with quality controls.
- **Staff:** Uses AI tools to improve throughput while maintaining quality and security.
- **Sr Staff:** Establishes AI usage patterns and guardrails for teams.
- **Principal:** Defines org-wide AI-native engineering practices and governance.

## Readiness Signals by Level

### Senior Readiness
- Owns a meaningful subsystem or product area end-to-end.
- Consistently delivers without detailed direction.
- Regularly improves team practices through technical leadership.

### Staff Readiness
- Work spans multiple teams with clear technical authority.
- Sought out for complex technical decisions.
- Creates leverage through reusable systems or processes.

### Principal Readiness
- Owns strategy for a cross-team domain over multi-quarter horizon.
- Sought out for highest-ambiguity, highest-impact technical decisions.
- Demonstrates durable organizational improvements, not one-off heroics.
- Explains tradeoffs clearly to both engineers and senior stakeholders.
