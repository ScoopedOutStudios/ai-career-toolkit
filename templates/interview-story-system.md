# Interview Story System

Map core stories to common interview loops and refine them systematically.

## Story Structure (Use for Every Answer)

1. **Context:** What customer problem existed and why it mattered to the business.
2. **Ambiguity:** What was unclear or contested.
3. **Decision:** What technical or strategic choices you made.
4. **Influence:** How you aligned stakeholders across functions.
5. **Outcome:** Measurable impact and what changed for the organization.
6. **Reflection:** What you would improve next time.

## Story Bank Template

Create stories for each of these common interview themes. Aim for 6-8 stories that cover all themes with overlap.

### Story 1 — Architecture / System Design
- **Best for:** Principal architecture, strategic influence, product sense.
- **Core point:** <!-- How you shaped a major technical direction -->
- **Outcome:** <!-- Measurable architecture or system impact -->

### Story 2 — Reliability / Operational Excellence
- **Best for:** Systems design, reliability, scalability.
- **Core point:** <!-- How you built or improved a system at scale -->
- **Outcome:** <!-- Availability, performance, or incident metrics -->

### Story 3 — Ambiguous Platform Problem
- **Best for:** Ambiguous platform design, cross-org coordination.
- **Core point:** <!-- How you defined and solved a problem with unclear scope -->
- **Outcome:** <!-- How the solution scaled or was adopted -->

### Story 4 — Cross-Team Incident / Quality Leadership
- **Best for:** Operational excellence, judgment under pressure.
- **Core point:** <!-- How you stabilized systems and improved guardrails -->
- **Outcome:** <!-- Incident frequency, severity, or MTTR improvement -->

### Story 5 — AI / Innovation in Practice
- **Best for:** Innovation, execution strategy, engineering productivity.
- **Core point:** <!-- Practical AI or tooling adoption with quality guardrails -->
- **Outcome:** <!-- Cycle-time or throughput gains with stable defect rate -->

### Story 6 — Mentorship / Sponsorship
- **Best for:** People leadership without formal authority.
- **Core point:** <!-- How you leveled up peers or tech leads -->
- **Outcome:** <!-- Promotions, ownership growth, or reduced bottlenecking -->

### Story 7 — Tradeoff Under Constraints
- **Best for:** Principal judgment and communication.
- **Core point:** <!-- Decision where delivery pressure conflicted with platform health -->
- **Outcome:** <!-- Clear tradeoff framing and measured impact -->

### Story 8 — Security / Compliance Strategy
- **Best for:** Security/reliability strategy, cross-functional leadership.
- **Core point:** <!-- Building trust, compliance, or security capabilities -->
- **Outcome:** <!-- Audit readiness, risk reduction, or controls automation -->

## Interview Loop Mapping

| Interview Type | Recommended Stories |
|---------------|-------------------|
| Systems Design / Architecture | 1, 2, 3, 7 |

| Execution / Delivery | 2, 4, 5 |
| Technical Strategy / Principal Panel | 1, 3, 5, 7 |

## Prepare Two Lengths

For each story, prepare:
- **2-minute version:** High signal, executive summary.
- **5-minute version:** Includes technical details, tradeoffs, and influence mechanics.

## Weekly Refinement Loop

After each interview:
1. Note which stories landed well and which felt weak.
2. Record objections or follow-up probes from interviewers.
3. Improve one sentence for clarity and one metric for credibility.
4. Add one explicit customer-outcome sentence if the story feels too engineering-internal.
5. Rehearse updated version before next loop.

## Hands-On Leadership Signal

In each leadership story, explicitly call out when you went hands-on versus when you delegated and enabled others. Frame this as deliberate leverage, not heroics.

## Red Flags To Avoid

- Over-indexing on implementation details without business context.
- Telling platform stories without clearly stating the customer problem.
- AI narrative that sounds like hype rather than disciplined engineering.
- Generic claims without specific, measurable outcomes.
