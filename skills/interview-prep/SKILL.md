# Interview Prep

## Purpose

Prepare the user to communicate impact clearly and perform confidently across interview rounds. Covers the full cycle: story building, mock practice with scoring, prep planning, and debrief.

## Use When

- Preparing for behavioral, systems design, or hiring manager interviews.
- Turning project notes into interview-ready STAR narratives.
- Running mock practice rounds with scoring and feedback.
- Building a prep plan for an upcoming panel.
- Debriefing after an interview to capture lessons.
- Filling gaps in story coverage (conflict, leadership, ambiguity, failure, influence).

## Modes

This skill operates in several modes depending on what the user needs:

### Story Building

Build compact, high-signal STAR stories that are easy to reuse across interview themes.

1. Capture role context and target interview level.
2. Draft STAR in 5-8 bullets.
3. Tighten to 60-90 second spoken answer.
4. Add one follow-up depth point and one metric anchor.

**Quality checks:**
- Action section reflects the user's personal decisions.
- Result section has measurable outcomes.
- No generic claims without proof.

**Output:** Story title, interview theme, STAR draft, 60-second spoken version, follow-up depth prompt.

### Mock Practice

Simulate interview rounds one question at a time, score performance, and improve answers through iteration.

1. Define round type and likely question themes.
2. Ask one question at a time and collect the answer.
3. Score on clarity, structure, evidence, scope/ownership signal, and executive presence (1-5 each).
4. Provide upgrade suggestions and a revised answer.
5. Repeat until the target score is reached or the round is complete.

**Output:** Per-question scorecard, what to keep / what to change, revised answer, next practice focus.

### Prep Planning

Create a structured preparation plan for an upcoming interview.

1. Identify interview format, rounds, and likely themes.
2. Select top STAR stories for each expected theme.
3. Identify gaps and build new stories if needed.
4. Produce a concise final prep brief with a day-of checklist.

**Output:** Round context and priorities, story set and talking points, practice plan, gap list and fixes, final day checklist.

### Debrief

Review how an interview went and extract lessons.

1. Capture what went well and what didn't.
2. Identify questions that were weak or surprising.
3. Suggest specific improvements for next time.
4. Update story bank with new material.

**Output:** Strengths, gaps, specific improvement actions, story bank updates.
