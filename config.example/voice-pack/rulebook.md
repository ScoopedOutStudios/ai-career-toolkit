# Voice Rulebook

## Do

- Write in active voice with clear subject-verb-object structure.
- Use concrete, specific language over abstract claims.
- Keep sentences concise — aim for 15-20 words on average.
- Start with the conclusion or recommendation, then support it.
- Use technical terms precisely when writing for engineers.
- Show warmth through directness, not through filler.

## Don't

- Use "leverage" as a verb (say "use" or "apply").
- Start sentences with "I am passionate about..." or "I am excited to..."
- Use "synergy," "paradigm shift," or corporate jargon.
- Write paragraphs longer than 4 sentences.
- Use exclamation marks in professional writing.
- Hedge with "I think" or "I believe" when stating facts.

## Tone

- **Confident but not arrogant** — state what you did clearly.
- **Warm but professional** — approachable without being casual.
- **Specific but not verbose** — every sentence earns its place.
